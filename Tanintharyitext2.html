Kachin State
Kachin State is the northernmost state of Myanmar. It is bordered by China to the north and east, Shan State to the south, and Sagaing Region and India to the west.
Kayah state
Kayah State, or Karenni State, is a state of Myanmar. Situated in eastern Myanmar, it is bounded on the north by Shan State, on the east by Thailand's Mae Hong Son Province.
Kayin State
Kayin State, formerly known as Karen State, is a state of Myanmar. The capital city is Hpa-An, also spelled Pa-An.
Chin State
Located at the western side of Myanmar with Hakha as its capital, the Chin State/Chin Hills stands tall at 3,053 metres, attracting mountaineers and hikers to visit Chin.
Mon State
Mon State is an administrative division of Myanmar. It lies between Kayin State to the east, the Andaman Sea to the west, Bago Region to the north and Tanintharyi Region to the south
Rakhine State
Rakhine State, formerly known as Arakan State, is a state in Myanmar. Situated on the western coast, it is bordered by Chin State to the north, Magway Region, Bago Region.
Shan State
Shan State is a state of Myanmar. Shan State borders China to the north, Laos to the east, and Thailand to the south, and five administrative divisions of Myanmar in the west.
Magway Region
Magway Region is an administrative division in central Myanmar. It is the second largest of Myanmar's seven divisions, with an area of 44,820 km².
Mandalay Region
Mandalay is a city and former royal capital in northern Myanmar (formerly Burma) on the Irrawaddy River.
Yangon Region
Yangon is the largest city in Myanmar.A mix of British colonial architecture, modern high-rises and gilded Buddhist pagodas define its skyline.
Sagaing Region
Sagaing is the former capital of the Sagaing Region of Myanmar. It is located on the Irrawaddy River, 20 km to the south-west of Mandalay on the opposite bank of the river.
Tanintharyi Region
Tanintharyi Region is a region of Myanmar, covering the long narrow southern part of the country on the northern Malay Peninsula, reaching to the Kra Isthmus.
Ayeyarwady Region
Ayeyawady Region is a region of Myanmar, occupying the delta region of the Ayeyarwady River.
Bago Region
Bago Region is an administrative region of Myanmar, located in the southern central part of the country.

Myeik Archipelago

The Myeik Archipelago, located off the coast of the Tanintharyi Region in southern Myanmar, is a vast group of over 800 islands, stretching across the Andaman Sea. Known for its stunning, remote beauty, the archipelago offers diverse landscapes, from lush forests and limestone cliffs to pristine beaches and rich coral reefs.The archipelago’s waters are teeming with marine life, including rare species of fish, turtles, and coral, making it a paradise for snorkeling, diving, and marine conservation efforts.The Myeik Archipelago remains one of Southeast Asia’s last untouched paradises, attracting travelers with its natural beauty, tranquil beaches, and unique culture.

Nyaung Oo Phee Island

Nyaung Oo Phee Island is located in the southern part of Myanmar, specifically in the Myeik Archipelago of the Tanintharyi Region. It's situated close to the border with Thailand and is known for its stunning natural beauty, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life.This hidden gem in the Myeik Archipelago is a tropical paradise, renowned for its lush coral reefs, pristine white-sand beaches, and vibrant marine life. Whether you’re snorkeling through colorful reefs, lounging by the shore, or exploring the nearby islets, Nyaung Oo Phee offers an unforgettable escape into nature’s serenity. With its secluded location, the island is the perfect retreat for those seeking peace, adventure, and natural splendor. Discover the captivating allure of Nyaung Oo Phee Island and create memories that will last a lifetime!".

Activities on Nyaung Oo Phee Island

Nyaung Oo Phee Island offers a range of activities for visitors to enjoy. You can explore vibrant coral reefs through snorkeling and diving, relax on pristine beaches, and paddle around the coastline in a kayak. Hiking scenic trails provides stunning views, while camping allows you to experience the island's natural beauty at night. Photography enthusiasts can capture breathtaking landscapes and sunsets, and wildlife watchers can spot various bird species and marine life. Engaging with local communities and savoring traditional food adds a cultural touch to your visit. Overall, the island is a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure in a stunning natural setting.
