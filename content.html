Van Kio Museum ( Surkhua town )

Hakha township, the Capital City

Hakha is the capital city of Chin State and it is located in the western part of Myanmar. The city is one of the coldest cities in Myanmar as it is situated 1,870m above sea level. Hakha is a must place to visit for those beautiful scenery and ancient buildings and a splendid view to take photographs in person. Major tourist attractions in Hakha are Hakha Viewpoint (1) & (2), Chin Chief House, Hakha Baptist Church, Chin Cultural Museum, Aidii Weaving House, Timit valley and the local market. The city can be reached by direct express bus from Yangon / Mandalay and flight via Kalay town.

The Bankiyo Museum is located in Surkhua, Hakkha Township, Chin State, and is easily accessible via the Hakkha Matupi main road. Built in 1920, the house of U Bankiyo has now been standing for over 100 years and is recognized as a significant traditional Chin house. Major traditional festivals of the Chin people are often held at this residence, and U Bankiyo is a descendant of the clan that has governed Surkhua since its establishment. He took over the leadership role from his father, U Mankone, in 1910. In 1914, he was appointed as the chief of the Surkhua Tribal Area by the British colonial administration, and in 1934, he received gifts from the Burmese government. oday, the Bankiyo Museum preserves U Bankiyo's residence, allowing visitors to explore the history and cultural artifacts of the Chin people, making it an important site for travelers.
